CLUTTER As a T( )L: [An Index for Messy Pedagogy]

[Thesis] [Editorial] [Research]

Clutter is everywhere. Many perceive clutter as a physical mess that is the evidence of countless delayed decisions, but how can clutter exist in our mental space? For artists and designers who often overwhelm themselves with the creative process, mental clutter usually accumulates because of the need of soul search within their practice and the ambiguity of self–direction that is often required for creatives.

Completeness is what people instinctively strive for, yet it is so feared that what was meant to end, is kept unfinished. “CLUTTER As A T( )L” is a curation of 100+ art and design topics/imagery that is scattered throughout the book with seemingly no direction and intention. Each topic is divided into pieces of information each identified by an object number and a key that leads you to the next piece of information. These information loops continue alongside each other, even momentarily interacting with one another, to create a defined, undefined system throughout the book form, visually representing clutter through information and physically exhibiting the endlessness of clutter via 5 section dos–a–dos book form.
