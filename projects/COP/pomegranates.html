The C*lour of Pomegranates

[Editorial Design]

How can the asterisk be used as a symbol of censorship?

When typing a password into a website, the asterisk is often used to shield or hide what is being inputted. This book features the The Color of Pomegranates, a Soviet-Armenian film by Sergei Parajanov, which depicts the life of 18th-century poet and musician Sayat Nova. Sayat Nova's life is shown in eight chapters through visual tableaux. Due to heavy suppression by the Soviet Union, many aspects of this film were changed or scrubbed, and the asterisks serve as a visual depiction of that erasure. The use of red string as binding serves as a symbol of strength and protection that is also seen throughout the film.

Whether the asterisk is used for silence or through its many other interpretations, this publication utilizes the symbol as a means to dehumanize the human face. This provides a direct correlation to the heavy censorship that affected the film during this time period.
